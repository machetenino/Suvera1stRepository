What we do.

We work through a small number of clear engagement models. The project type may change. Our standard does not.

Engagement models
01

Construction
Management

We hold the full delivery scope on behalf of the client. Programme, procurement, consultant coordination, trade engagement, site management and handover — all carried by one firm. We are not managing on the periphery. We are running the project, and we are answerable for it.

This is the engagement model for clients who need the project held, not supervised at a distance. One firm responsible for every decision, every interface, every outcome.

02

Head
Contracting

We execute the head contract and take on full legal and operational responsibility for the scope — subcontractor engagement, programme delivery, compliance, documentation and the defects liability period. We hold the contract, and we are answerable for what it requires.

Not a manager reporting on progress. A contractor responsible for the delivered result — from commencement through to end of the defects period.

03

Early Contractor
Involvement

We engage during design to provide construction input before the programme is locked. Buildability review, trade market assessment, programme structuring, and cost reality checks — delivered while decisions can still change, not after they have been built around.

ECI closes the gap between what is designed and what gets built. It protects design intent by surfacing constraints early, and it gives the full project team a grounded construction position before contracts are executed.

04

Design &
Construct

Where the brief and consultant structure require it, we hold both the design and construction scope. We engage and manage the design team, carry the design risk, and remain accountable for how the project is delivered against the brief.

This model is applied where it is the right fit — not as a default, and not in a way that compromises the relationship between the client and their designers. Where an interior designer or architect is already part of the project, our role is to build what they have designed, to the standard they intended.

Additional scopes

Make Good &
Tenancy Works

End-of-lease reinstatement, make good obligations and new tenancy fitout works. Often delivered inside occupied or partially occupied buildings, against landlord programmes and lease conditions. Managed with the same rigour as our primary scope work.

Occupied
Environments

We work inside operating businesses. Construction staging, noise and access management, sequencing around live areas, and coordination with building management — planned in advance, not improvised on site. The people working around us should not notice us more than necessary.

Base Building
Upgrades

Lobby upgrades, end-of-trip facilities, plant and services replacement, and common area refurbishment. Delivered to base building standard, within building management constraints, with programme certainty and documented handover to the asset owner.
